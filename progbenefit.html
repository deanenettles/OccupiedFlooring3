  • Centralized project management oversight

Outcomes:

  • Uniform quality across states
  • Reduced schedule variance
  • Controlled capital deployment

Reduced Operational Risk

Designed for Occupied Federal Environments

Traditional renovation introduces operational disruption through:

  • Furniture disassembly
  • Electrical and data system exposure
  • Trade overlap
  • Extended downtime
  • Increased sequencing conflict

OFS eliminates these risk factors through a controlled occupied methodology:

  • Workstations lifted—not dismantled
  • Electrical and IT systems remain live
  • Night and off-hour execution protocols
  • Integrated flooring and painting coordination
  • Defined logistics and inventory management

Outcomes:

  • Protected business continuity
  • Lower total project disruption
  • Reduced operational exposure
