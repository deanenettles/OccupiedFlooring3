The OFS Execution Model: Controlled Renovation in Active Federal Environments

Occupied Flooring Solutions (OFS) has developed a standardized execution framework specifically engineered for occupied government and institutional facilities.

Our methodology eliminates unnecessary disruption while preserving operational continuity, electrical integrity, and schedule control.

This is not traditional renovation.
It is structured occupied execution.

Traditional Renovation Model

High Disruption. Fragmented Oversight. Increased Risk.

Conventional flooring replacement in active environments typically requires:

  • Furniture disassembly and reinstallation
  • Electrical and data system exposure
  • Multiple subcontractor coordination
  • Extended downtime
  • Trade sequencing conflicts
  • Increased operational risk

Each additional trade introduces scheduling complexity, cost escalation, and business interruption exposure.

The OFS Occupied Methodology

Lift. Sequence. Restore. Operate.

OFS utilizes a controlled lift-and-replace system designed for mission-critical environments.

Phase 1 — Controlled Preparation
  • Employees secure personal items in labeled mobile containers
  • Detailed phasing plans define workspace sequencing
  • Asset and location tracking established
  • Logistics staging coordinated in advance
Phase 2 — Lift-and-Replace Execution
  • Workstations and cubicles lifted — not dismantled
  • Electrical and IT systems remain live
  • Flooring removed and replaced within defined micro-phases
  • Integrated painting (if applicable) executed within same controlled sequence
Phase 3 — Verification & Return to Service
  • Quality control inspection
  • Square footage verification
  • Closeout documentation
  • Spaces returned fully operational the next business day

This structured methodology minimizes downtime while preserving system integrity.

Why the OFS Process Reduces Risk
